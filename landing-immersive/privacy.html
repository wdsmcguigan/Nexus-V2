01 Scope of this policy

This policy applies only to the NEXUS marketing website. It does not govern the NEXUS desktop or mobile application itself, which is a local-first email client that stores your data on your own device. See Section 06 for a summary of how the application handles data, and the in-app documentation for full detail.

By using the Site, you acknowledge the practices described here. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Site.

02 Information we collect

The Site is a static website. We do not operate accounts, forms, comment systems, or trackers on it, so we do not directly collect personal information from you when you browse.

Automatically processed server logs

Like virtually all websites, our hosting provider automatically processes a limited amount of technical data each time a page is requested, in order to deliver the Site and protect it from abuse. This may include:

  • Your IP address (often truncated or anonymized by the provider);
  • Browser type and version, and operating system;
  • The page requested, referring page, and a timestamp.

This information is generated as part of the standard operation of internet infrastructure, is used only for security, diagnostics, and delivery, and is not used by us to build profiles or identify you.

03 How we use information

To the extent any technical data is processed, we (and our hosting provider on our behalf) use it solely to:

  • Serve the Site and its assets to your browser;
  • Maintain the security, integrity, and performance of the Site;
  • Diagnose technical problems and prevent abuse.

We do not sell or rent personal information, use it for advertising, share it with data brokers, or use automated decision-making or profiling.

04 Cookies & tracking

The Site does not use cookies, local storage for tracking, web beacons, fingerprinting, or any analytics. Because we set no non-essential cookies, no consent banner is required. For details, see our Cookie Policy.

06 The NEXUS application & your data

The application is local-first by design. Your mail lives in an encrypted SQLite vault on your own device. Optional cross-device sync is end-to-end encrypted through a self-hosted relay that only ever stores ciphertext — it cannot read your mail. We do not host your email or hold your encryption keys.

This summary is provided for transparency. The application is governed by its own in-product terms and documentation, not by this website policy.

07 Data retention

We do not maintain a database of Site visitors. Server logs processed by our hosting provider are retained only for as long as needed for security and operational purposes, in accordance with that provider’s retention practices, and are then deleted or anonymized.

08 Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights over your personal information. Because we collect essentially no personal information through the Site, in most cases there is little or no data for us to act on — but we honor these rights regardless.

EEA, UK & Switzerland (GDPR)

You may have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to processing of your personal data, and to data portability. Where we process limited technical data, our legal basis is our legitimate interest in operating and securing the Site (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR). You may also lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

California (CCPA/CPRA)

California residents may have the right to know, delete, and correct personal information, and to opt out of its “sale” or “sharing.” We do not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined, and we do not process sensitive personal information for inferring characteristics.

To exercise any right, contact us at mediaabsurdity@gmail.com. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

09 International visitors

The Site is operated from the United States and hosted on global infrastructure. If you access it from outside the United States, your request data may be processed in the United States or other countries where our hosting provider operates, which may have different data-protection laws than your jurisdiction.

10 Children’s privacy

The Site is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

11 Security

The Site is served over HTTPS. While no method of transmission over the internet is perfectly secure, our minimal-data approach is itself a security measure: the less we collect, the less there is to protect or expose.
