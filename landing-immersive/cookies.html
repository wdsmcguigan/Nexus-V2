01 What cookies are

Cookies are small text files that a website can store on your device to remember information about you — for example, login state, preferences, or analytics identifiers. “First-party” cookies are set by the site you are visiting; “third-party” cookies are set by other domains (often advertising or analytics networks).

02 Our use of cookies

We use none. The Site is a static website that does not set first-party or third-party cookies. The table below is intentionally short:

CategoryUsed?Details
Strictly necessaryNoneThe Site functions without storing any cookie on your device.
PreferencesNoneNo settings are persisted via cookies.
Analytics / performanceNoneWe run no analytics and collect no usage statistics.
Advertising / targetingNoneNo ad networks, no tracking pixels, no remarketing.

03 Similar technologies

We also do not use the cookie-adjacent technologies that commonly track visitors, including:

  • Local / session storage for tracking or identification;
  • Web beacons or tracking pixels (e.g., invisible images that report opens);
  • Device or browser fingerprinting;
  • Third-party embeds that would silently load trackers. Notably, all fonts are self-hosted, so your browser never contacts a font CDN such as Google Fonts.

04 Why there’s no consent banner

Laws such as the EU/UK ePrivacy rules and GDPR require consent before storing or accessing non-essential information on your device. Because we store nothing and access nothing on your device, the legal basis for a consent banner does not arise. We’d rather earn your trust by not tracking you than by asking permission to.

If we ever introduce cookies or analytics in the future, we will update this policy first and present a genuine, granular consent choice before any non-essential technology runs.

05 Controlling cookies in your browser

Even though we set none, you remain in full control of cookies generally. Most browsers let you view, block, or delete cookies via their settings. Helpful references:

  • Google Chrome
  • Apple Safari
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
